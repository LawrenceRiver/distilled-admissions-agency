# Scoring Rubric

Scores are decision aids, not probabilities. Do not present a numeric score as an admission chance.

## Applicant Polygon

Use two main axes plus sub-dimensions.

### Hard Strength

| Dimension | Weight | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| GPA and rank | 35 | Position against minimum, typical cases, and transcript trend |
| Coursework/prerequisites | 25 | Match required math, CS, design, stats, or domain courses |
| Tests/language | 15 | TOEFL/IELTS/GRE/GMAT relative to requirement and competitive range |
| Institution context | 10 | Undergraduate institution, grading context, exchange/dual-degree signals |
| Field baseline | 15 | Whether the undergraduate major is direct, adjacent, or distant |

### Soft Strength

| Dimension | Weight | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Projects/products | 25 | Built systems, shipped products, technical depth, users |
| Research/papers/patents | 20 | Research fit, invention, lab work, publications, patents |
| Awards/competitions | 15 | Level, relevance, selectivity |
| Professional/social practice | 15 | Internships, startup, social impact, leadership |
| Portfolio/story | 15 | Design/music/creative assets, narrative distinctiveness |
| Recommender potential | 10 | Credible recommenders who can verify the story |

## Program Fit Dimensions

| Dimension | Weight | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Requirement fit | 30 | Hard gate, GPA, prerequisites, tests |
| Curriculum fit | 20 | Courses and labs match applicant direction |
| Narrative fit | 20 | Applicant value proposition fits program identity |
| Outcome fit | 10 | Career/research goals match outcomes |
| Case similarity | 10 | Public cases resemble applicant profile |
| Tolerance signal | 10 | Holistic review, interdisciplinary cohort, portfolio-friendly admissions |

Route-map evidence can adjust the qualitative band by one step when it is strong and well-sourced. It must not override hard gates.

## Banding Rules

Use these bands:

- `high-reach`: hard profile below typical range, selective program, or weak direct fit; possible only with exceptional soft story.
- `reach`: competitive but meaningful risk; at least one of GPA, selectivity, prerequisites, or portfolio is stretched.
- `main`: realistic target where hard profile is plausible and soft story can matter.
- `main-stable`: stronger main target; hard requirements are met, fit is clean, and public cases do not look far above applicant.
- `safety`: requirements are comfortably met and selectivity/cases suggest low relative risk. Use sparingly for competitive U.S. graduate programs.
- `not-advised`: narrative mismatch, hard gate conflict, poor ROI, or user constraints conflict.
- `blocked`: missing GPA, missing official requirements, or insufficient evidence.

## Hard Gates

If any official hard gate is unmet, mark `not-advised` or `blocked` unless the official source states exceptions.

Hard gates include:

- minimum GPA
- mandatory prerequisite degree or courses
- mandatory portfolio/writing sample the applicant cannot provide
- English test minimum
- citizenship/residency restriction
- deadline already passed for the relevant cycle

## GPA Risk

Classify GPA for every program:

- `low`: applicant GPA is at/above typical admitted range or no risk signal found.
- `medium`: applicant GPA is above minimum but below visible competitive cases.
- `high`: applicant GPA is near minimum, below common public cases, or program is known for high academic selectivity.
- `unknown`: no official or public signal.

## Soft Compensation

Soft strength can compensate for medium GPA risk when:

- official language emphasizes holistic review
- portfolio/project/research is central to evaluation
- public cases show non-perfect GPA admits with strong projects
- applicant's work directly matches program labs/curriculum

Soft strength cannot erase hard gates.

## Tolerance / "玄学" Factor

Translate "玄学" into observable admissions tolerance. Score 0-20 internally:

- holistic review language: 0-4
- interdisciplinary or non-traditional cohort: 0-4
- portfolio/project-heavy evaluation: 0-4
- flexible prerequisites: 0-4
- public special cases: 0-4

Report it as `tolerance signal`, not as fate or certainty.

## School Route Fit

Score the applicant against the route hypothesis:

- `route-core`: the applicant fits the dominant cohort archetype and evidence language.
- `route-bridge`: the applicant is not a standard profile but has a defensible bridge.
- `route-gamble`: the applicant needs the school to reward unusual evidence.
- `route-mismatch`: the applicant's story fights the program identity.

Use this to explain why two programs with similar rankings may have different fit bands.

## Evidence Confidence

Use:

- `high`: official requirements verified and at least several relevant cases or strong official program fit.
- `medium`: official requirements verified but cases are limited.
- `low`: requirements or cases are incomplete.
- `blocked`: core evidence missing.
